STATION wagon

A station wagon (US, also wagon) or estate car (UK, also estate) is an automotive body-style variant of a sedan with its roof extended rearward over a shared passenger/cargo volume with access at the back via a third or fifth door (the liftgate, or tailgate), instead of a trunk/boot lid.[1] The body style transforms a standard three-box design into a two-box designâ€”to include an A, B, and C-pillar, as well as a D-pillar.
